Overview

3 Animals in a Trench Coat is a 2D top-down puzzle adventure game with stealth elements that was built in Unity for an academic project. The player plays as three animals stacked on top of each other in a trench coat to pretend to be a human. I was responsible for the level design, environmental art, sound design and system, as well as being involved in the game designing process.

Process

I was responsible for the level design and contributed to the scenarios and puzzles the player would face. In designing the environments, my focus was on ensuring that players knew where they had to go and what was interactable or a point of interest. This was important because clues to many of our puzzles were embedded into the environment or required the player to “investigate” specific areas of the map.

To do this, I made points of interests “pop out” of the environment more through the use of contrasting colours, like the paintings in Figure 1, or through placing interesting objects or areas, like the desk with papers.

Feedback

We conducted playtesting sessions where we received feedback where one of the puzzles in level 3 was too difficult and that the visual hint (the image on the divider) was too small to make out its details. Also, the difficulty of the word related puzzles decreased going from level 3 to 4 instead of either remaining the same or increasing.

To resolve the issues I changed the imagery in the divider, as seen below, so the visuals are clearer to the player through its connection to the text hint as the previous text hints did not mention the drawn animals. The divider decreased to 2 columns so the player could make out their details better. In addition, the shelf also got lowered so it wouldn’t block the image.

Level 3’s text hints were based on predator-prey relationships but the text hints didn’t relate to the divider image very well so they were reworded to involve the animals on the divider because players were unable to figure out the theme of this puzzle. It was also told to the player more explicitly that the password referred to predator and prey instead of relying on pure imagery.

Level 4’s puzzle was reworded from telling the player a number to hiding the number within a sentence to improve our difficulty ramping.

Reflection

During this project, I learned the importance of playtesting throughout each stage of the project. With each playtesting session, new issues would show up involving player understanding and intuition which resulted in modifications having to be made based on feedback. It prevented us from having to create larger changes down the line as we were able to deal with issues as they cropped up.

In terms of level design, it helped us improve the player’s experience as they progressed through our game because we were able to identify areas of confusion quickly and resolve them.
